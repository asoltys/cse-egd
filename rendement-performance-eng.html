Performance Standard

At the Esquimalt Graving Dock (EGD), Public Works and Government Services Canada (PWGSC) will provide in a reasonable manner:

  • administration services, to reserve services or space for vessels
  • notification of uses within 48 hours of changes to service/space that may affect the safe and efficient operations of the facility;
  • an Emergency Response Plan for the EGD; and
  • an approved marine facility security plan and a qualified Marine Facility Security Officer.

PWGSC will report on the measurement of the services provided on an annual basis.

  • Reservations and confirmed bookings were provided in a reasonable manner when requested.
  • Notifications of changes to service and/or capacity of the facility will be provided within 48 hours after first noting the change in condition of the service or capacity at least 90% of the time.
  • The EGD Emergency Response Plan will be reviewed and updated, if required, every 2 years.
  • The EGD will continue to have a valid Statement of Compliance of Marine Facility or a Port certificate and that all regulated security audits have been completed.
  • Disputes related to service and/or capacity of the EGD would be handled in the following manner:
First level of referral:
Director, Esquimalt Graving Dock
Second level of referral:
Director General Engineering Asset Strategy
Directorate
Third level of referral:
Assistant Deputy Minister – Real Property
Associate Deputy Minister – PWGSC

Note: this document will be updated annually.
