Pumphouse Staff

The pumphouse staff are responsible for, amongst other things, filling-up and emptying the drydock. They also provide the following services:

  • Electrical
  • Mechanical
  • Engineering
  • Pipe fitting
  • Millright
  • Oilspill response
  • Fire and emergency response
  • Multi-Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition system (M-Scada)
  • High mast lighting
  • Auto lubricating system for main pumps
  • Lock-up and safety procedures
  • Compressed air
  • Sewage
  • Domestic potable water
  • Docking assistance with the Esquimalt II
  • Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMSS)

Filling-up and Emptying The Drydock

To drain the basin dock three 54" twin parallel centrifugal pumps are used. These pumps are each rated at 66,000 Imperial gallons per minute (I.G.P.M). There is also two 20" submerged auxiliary pumps which are each rated at 9,000 I.G.P.M. Two heavy-duty submersible pumps are used in addition to a hydraulic gate system or penstock.

Compressed Air

Compressed air service is provided through 1000 cubic feet per minute compressors. Compressed air piping and distribution services are comprised of a main line and four inch distribution lines at the North Landing Wharf, South Jetty and Graving Dock.


Top of Page

Electrical

Electrical services in the yard include 1500 amp splitters, 480V, 400A splitters and 120V duplex plug services as well as telephone and cable television services. Emergency service is provided by a 475VA diesel powered generator set.


Top of Page

Sewage

Sewer service is provided through a four inch main pumped through a sewage lift station. Site drainage is through a 24 inch main with catch-basins and sewers.


Top of Page

Yard Staff

The yard staff provide a variety of services including keel block setting, assisting with the docking and undocking vessels and maintaining the grounds on this 19 hectare site.
