Ship Repair Services

The Dock (which has a capacity of up to 100,000 DWT), is serviced by three heavy-lift electrically-driven, rail-mounted travelling cranes:

  • Krupp Level Luffing Crane – 150-tonne capacity at 35 metres and a 100-tonne capacity at 50 metres. This crane has a main hook capable of lifting entire vessels onto land for refit or repair and an auxiliary hook with a 15 tonne capacity generally used to transport materials onto the vessel.
  • Colby Hammerhead Crane – 45-tonne capacity at 30 metres. This crane has a main hook capable of 39-tonnes and a 9-tonne capacity auxiliary.
  • Ebco Level Luffing Crane – This quick maneuvering crane travels the full length of the Dock and has a 30-tonne capacity at 35 metres.

In addition, the Esquimalt Graving Dock has two mobile cranes – the 20-tonne Grove and a 12-tonne Hyster with boom lowering characteristics.

Crane services include a Hyster forklift truck and mobile crane. Kee blocks, wedges, fillers and timbers are also available.

Enviro Blast Ship Cleaning Service

These machines were developed specifically for use on steel horizontal and vertical surfaces like ship hulls, decks and holding/storage tanks. This is how it works:

Abrasive cleaning material is "thrown" against the surface to be cleaned using centrifugal force from a wheel revolving at high speed – effectively blast cleaning the area. The energy used to do this is sufficient enough that the abrasive material rebounds from the work surface into a reclaim duct for reuse.

Source:  Harbour & Shipping Journal, July 2002, Volume 85, No. 7
